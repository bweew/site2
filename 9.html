    block chord harmony

    c6 and ddim. harmonize the major scale by alternating between the inversions. also works for minor if you flatten the 3. this is the barry harris thing. the interesting part about this is 4 other keys share the same d dim. eb, f#, a. notice how these are notes of c diminished. there is a strong 1 5 pull with this kind of harmony. normal major harmony goes from completly resolved at 1 and moves towards the peak tension at 5 before coming back home, while with diminished theory the 2 is already max tension. this is because every diminished chord can be thought of as a rootless 5 chord relating to its other keys(a 7b9).

    am6 > e7b9 cm6 > g7b9 ebm6 > bb7b9 f#m6 > b7b9

    one dim shape is good for 8 keys. 4 major and 4 minor. this makes it worthwhile to commit it to muscle memory. becauae its shape is symetrical its also a little bit easier to visualize its placement on your insturment compared to other chord shapes.

    because there are only 3 diminished chords for all 12 keys and the rest are just inversions of themselves. by process of elimination if you just learn 1/3 you already narrow down majority of the notes you know to avoid or can lean into. this lets you develop your intuition as you improvise as you can hear what sounds out and make more connections.
